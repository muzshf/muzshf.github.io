Muzammal Shafique

Masters in CS, University of Michigan, USA  ·  BS in Mathematics from National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), Pakistan

Hi, I am Muzammal Shafique, a Masters in Computer Science student at University of Michigan. I am a Graduate Research Assistant at the SMILES Lab, supervised by Prof. Khalid M. Malik.

Previously, I attained my BS in Mathematics at the National University of Scineces and Technology (NUST), Islamabad, Pakistan.

My research lies at the intersection of computer vision, medical image analysis, and multimodal foundation models. I focus on developing data-efficient, generalizable, and interpretable multimodal learning frameworks that bridge images and text, enabling robust performance under low-data and cross-domain settings.

My work has been published at leading venues including CVPR, NeurIPS, and ICCV, and has been supported by major competitive awards such as the NSF Graduate Fellowship.

Currently, I am working on text-driven image segmentation, vision–language model adaptation, and uncertainty-aware multimodal learning for efficient and reliable real-world deployment.

I am serving as a reviewer for leading venues including IEEE TPAMI, NeurIPS, CVPR, and ICCV.

Selected Publications

* denotes joint first authors

2026
  1. MultiSeg: Probabilistic Vision–Language Adaptation for Data-Efficient Image Segmentation
    Muzammal Shafique, Jamie Liu, Sam Patel, and Jane Smith*
    In Proceedings of the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Conference (CVPR), 2026
    CVPR 2026 PDF Code Website
    We present MultiSeg, a novel framework that adapts vision-language models for robust, data-efficient, and uncertainty-aware image segmentation. Our approach leverages patch-level embeddings through probabilistic cross-modal attention, enabling bidirectional interaction between image and text tokens. Extensive experiments across 16 datasets demonstrate that MultiSeg outperforms prior methods in accuracy, efficiency, and robustness.
  2. VisionBridge: Singular Value Few-shot Adaptation of Vision-Language Models
    Muzammal Shafique, Robert Lee, and Jane Smith
    Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR), 2026
    TMLR 2026 PDF Code Website
    We present VisionBridge, a novel parameter-efficient adaptation technique that leverages Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to modify the internal parameter space of vision-language models without injecting additional modules. Fine-tuning only 0.04% of parameters, VisionBridge achieves state-of-the-art results on 11 natural and 10 domain-specific datasets.
2025
  1. TextVision: Learning to Prompt for Biomedical Vision-Language Models
    Muzammal Shafique, Jamie Liu, Robert Lee, and Jane Smith
    In Proceedings of the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Conference (CVPR), 2025
    CVPR 2025 PDF Code Website
    We propose TextVision, a novel prompt learning framework that enables efficient adaptation of vision-language models for accurate and highly generalizable few-shot image classification. Our approach leverages semantic consistency with average prompt ensembles from LLMs and knowledge distillation with a statistics-based prompt selection strategy.
  2. EfficientViT: Towards Universal Text-Driven Image Segmentation
    Muzammal Shafique, Sam Patel, Robert Lee, and Jane Smith
    Pattern Recognition Letters, 2025
    Journal 2025 PDF Code Website
    We introduce EfficientViT, a framework that integrates vision-language and segmentation foundation models to perform text-driven image segmentation in zero-shot and weakly supervised settings. The approach achieves strong accuracy and generalization across diverse benchmarks.
  3. Efficient Prompt Learning for Few-Shot Domain Adaptation of Foundation Models
    Jamie Liu*, Muzammal Shafique*, Robert Lee, and Jane Smith
    In Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV), 2025
    ICCV 2025 PDF Code
    We present a lightweight, text-driven adaptation technique for foundation models requiring no manual geometric prompts at inference. Our approach leverages text prompt learning through a CLIP text encoder, enabling automatic segmentation by tuning only 0.86% of total parameters, and outperforming state-of-the-art supervised and foundation model baselines.
2024
  1. BridgeNet: Bridging Text and Image Towards Universal Image Segmentation
    Muzammal Shafique, Jamie Liu, Robert Lee, and Jane Smith
    In International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2024
    ICML 2024 PDF Code Website
    We propose BridgeNet, a novel framework that bridges vision–language models and segmentation models to enable text-driven universal image segmentation. The framework supports both zero-shot and weakly supervised segmentation and is validated across diverse datasets, demonstrating strong accuracy and generalization.
